Netflix Data Analysis Dashboard 

The Netflix Data Analysis Dashboard is an interactive Power BI dashboard developed to analyze Netflix's global content library. It provides valuable insights into the distribution of movies and TV shows, their release trends, content categories, and country-wise availability. The dashboard helps users understand Netflix's content strategy and identify patterns through interactive visualizations.

Dashboard Overview

The dashboard contains 8,807 Netflix titles, which include both movies and TV shows.
Total Titles: 8,807
Movies: 6,131 (69.62%)
TV Shows: 2,676 (30.38%)
The analysis clearly indicates that movies constitute the majority of Netflix's content, while TV shows account for nearly one-third of the platform's library.

Dashboard Components
1. KPI Cards

The dashboard begins with three KPI cards that provide a quick summary of the dataset.
Total Content: Displays the total number of Netflix titles available in the dataset.
Movies: Shows the total number of movies.
TV Shows: Displays the total number of television series.
These KPIs offer an immediate understanding of Netflix's overall content distribution.

2. Movies vs TV Shows

A donut chart compares the proportion of movies and TV shows.
Insights:
Movies represent 69.62% of the total content.
TV Shows account for 30.38%.
This visualization highlights Netflix's greater focus on movie content while maintaining a substantial collection of TV series.

3. Netflix Content by Country

This bar chart illustrates the number of Netflix titles available across different countries.
Purpose
Identifies countries contributing the largest amount of content.
Helps compare content production and availability among countries.
Reveals Netflix's strong global presence.
Countries such as the United States, India, Canada, United Kingdom, and Japan contribute significantly to the content library.

4. Content Release Trend by Year

This visualization displays the number of titles released each year.
Insights
Very few titles were released before the early 2000s.
A significant increase in content production is observed after 2015.
The highest concentration of titles appears in recent years, reflecting Netflix's rapid expansion and investment in original content.
This chart demonstrates Netflix's continuous growth and increasing production over time.

5. Top Netflix Categories

This horizontal bar chart ranks the most common content categories.
Some of the leading categories include:
Dramas, International Movies
Documentaries
Stand-Up Comedy
Comedies
Dramas, Independent Movies
Kids' TV
Children's & Family Movies
Insights
Drama-based content dominates the platform.
Documentary and comedy genres also have a strong presence.
Family-friendly and children's content forms an important segment of Netflix's catalog.

6. Interactive Filters (Slicers)

The dashboard includes filters that allow users to customize the analysis.
Type Filter
Users can filter between:
Movies
TV Shows
Country Filter
Allows analysis of content available from selected countries.
Release Year Filter
Users can focus on titles released between 1925 and 2021, making historical trend analysis easier.

Key Business Insights

Netflix hosts over 8,800 titles, indicating a vast and diverse content library.
Nearly 70% of the catalog consists of movies, showing Netflix's stronger emphasis on film content.
The platform has experienced rapid growth in content production over the last decade.
Drama is the most popular genre, followed by documentaries and comedy.
Netflix sources content from numerous countries, demonstrating its global reach and commitment to diverse entertainment.
Interactive filters enable users to analyze content by country, release year, and content type for more specific insights.

Conclusion

The Netflix Data Analysis Dashboard provides a comprehensive overview of Netflix's global content library by examining content type, release trends, genres, and geographical distribution. The dashboard shows that movies dominate the platform, drama remains the most common genre, and content production has grown significantly in recent years. Through interactive filtering and intuitive visualizations, users can explore Netflix's catalog from multiple perspectives, making the dashboard valuable for business intelligence, data storytelling, and decision-making.
